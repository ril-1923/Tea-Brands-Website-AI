Traditional Teas

A Journey Through Centuries of Tea Culture and Heritage

Black Teas

Assam

A robust, malty tea from Northeast India. Known for its bold flavor and bright copper color, it's the perfect breakfast tea with strong character.

India

Ceylon

From the highlands of Sri Lanka, Ceylon tea offers a crisp, lively taste with citrus notes. Medium-bodied with a golden hue.

Sri Lanka

Darjeeling

The "Champagne of Teas" from the Himalayan foothills. Delicate, aromatic, with muscatel flavors and floral notes.

India

Keemun

A Chinese black tea with a smooth, wine-like character. Subtle fruity and floral notes with a slightly smoky finish.

China

Green Teas

Sencha

Japan's most popular green tea. Fresh, grassy flavor with a balance of sweetness and astringency. Steamed to preserve its vibrant green color.

Japan

Matcha

Ceremonial powdered green tea from Japan. Rich, creamy texture with umami depth. Packed with antioxidants and used in traditional tea ceremonies.

Japan

Longjing (Dragon Well)

One of China's most famous teas. Pan-fired for a distinctive flat shape. Sweet, nutty flavor with chestnut notes.

China

Gyokuro

Japan's highest grade of green tea. Shade-grown for three weeks before harvest, creating a sweet, mellow flavor with rich umami.

Japan

Oolong Teas

Tie Guan Yin (Iron Goddess)

A legendary Chinese oolong with floral aroma and creamy texture. Complex flavor that evolves through multiple infusions.

China

Da Hong Pao (Big Red Robe)

One of China's most precious teas from Wuyi Mountains. Rich, complex flavor with mineral notes and a lingering sweet finish.

China
